The Genius of Richard Feynman

    Here is a timeline of Feynman's life:

  • 1918 - Born in Far Rockaway, Queens, New York
  • 1939 - Receives a bachelor's degree from MIT
  • 1942-Receives a Ph.D. from Princeton University, working under John Archibald Wheeler
  • 1943-Joins the Manhattan Project at Los Alamos to work on the development of the atomic bomb
  • 1945-Witnesses the first detonation of an atomic bomb in the Trinity test
  • 1945 - Marries his first wife, Arline Greenbaum, who dies later that year
  • 1947 - Begins teaching theoretical physics at Cornell University
  • 1950 - Moves to Caltech (California Institute of Technology) to work as a professor of theoretical physics
  • 1959 - Delivers his famous lecture, "There's Plenty of Room at the Bottom," which discusses the potential of nanotechnology
  • 1961 - Publishes "Quantum Electrodynamics," which becomes a cornerstone in the field
  • 1965 - Receives the Nobel Prize in Physics, along with Julian Schwinger and Sin-Itiro Tomonaga, for their work in quantum electrodynamics
  • 1965 - Publishes "The Feynman Lectures on Physics," which becomes a highly influential series of physics textbooks
  • 1986 - Serves on the Rogers Commission to investigate the Space Shuttle Challenger disaster, famously demonstrating the effect of cold on the shuttle's O-rings with a simple experiment
  • 1988 - Dies of abdominal cancer at the age of 69 (nice)
